
The Concept of "Flow"

Both activities are frequently cited in studies regarding the Flow State, a psychological phenomenon where an individual becomes fully immersed in an activity.

In Dance: Flow is often achieved through the synchronization of movement with auditory stimuli (music).

In Swimming: Flow is linked to the repetitive, meditative nature of lap swimming and the sensory deprivation provided by being underwater.	

Technical Note on Cross-TrainingAthletes often use swimming as a recovery tool for dance-related injuries. Because water is roughly 800 times denser than air, it provides a full-body workout without the joint stress associated with jumps and "turn-out" in dance. Conversely, the core strength and disciplined posture of a dancer often translate into a more streamlined "torpedo" shape in the pool, reducing the coefficient of drag ($C_d$) during the glide phase of a stroke."To dance is to be out of yourself. To swim is to be inside your body in a different world."
